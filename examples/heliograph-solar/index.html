Process

Four steps, most of them ours

A commercial system typically runs 12 to 18 weeks from first site visit to energized, and the interconnection queue is the usual pacing item. We manage it end to end.

Week 1

Assess

Roof walk, structural review, shading study, and a pull of your utility interval data.

Weeks 2 to 5

Design

Stamped structural and electrical drawings, a production model, and the interconnection application filed.

Weeks 6 to 14

Install

Racking, modules, and inverters, usually four to eight weeks on the roof with your operations undisturbed.

Years 1 to 25

Monitor

Metered production checked against the model, with a report to you annually and alerts to us daily.

Incentives

The math is subsidized, and the subsidies are documented

Roughly half the cost of a commercial system in our territory is currently covered by federal and state programs. We model each one conservatively, and every line in your proposal cites the statute or program document it comes from.

Incentives change. Your proposal is dated, and we requote it if a program moves before you sign.

About

Engineers first, salespeople a distant second

Heliograph was started in 2021 by two electrical engineers who kept getting asked to fix underperforming arrays other companies had sold. Most of the fixes traced back to optimistic production models. So we model conservatively, meter everything, and publish our fleet numbers.

The company holds electrical and general building licenses, carries the insurance your facilities team will ask about, and self-performs all roof work.
