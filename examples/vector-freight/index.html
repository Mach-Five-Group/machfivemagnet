Intermodal

Long lanes belong on rail

If the lane runs 750 miles or more and the delivery window has a day of slack, intermodal usually wins on cost without losing the tracking discipline. We handle drayage on both ends, so it books and tracks like a truckload.

Carrier network

Vetted before the first load, watched after it

A big network is only useful if the trucks in it show up. Every carrier passes the same four gates, and staying in the network is conditional, not permanent.

  1. Authority and insurance verifiedOperating authority, cargo, and auto liability confirmed directly at onboarding, not from a screenshot.
  2. Safety record reviewedInspection history and out-of-service rates screened against network thresholds before first dispatch.
  3. Performance scored by laneOn-time, tracking compliance, and claims feed a per-lane score that decides who gets the next tender.
  4. Monitored continuouslyInsurance lapses and authority changes flag the same day and pause dispatch automatically.
